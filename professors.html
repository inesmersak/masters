Our department is large and diverse, with roughly 70 professors and teaching assistants, and we cover
all major fields of mathematics such as Complex Analysis, Global Analysis, Functional Analysis, Algebra, Topology, Numerical Mathematics, Graph Theory, Combinatorics, Theoretical Computer Science, Mechanics, Financial Mathematics, Probability and Statistics. We pride ourselves on being not just good mathematicians, but also friendly and accomodating to our students. Mathematics is hard enough as it is!

The department also organizes 11 weekly research seminars and a monthly colloqium.

Sandi Klavžar

Sandi Klavžar works in discrete mathematics. He has published 250 papers, co-authored several books including Product Graphs (Wiley, New York, 2000) and The Tower of Hanoi (Springer, Basel, 2013), and is an associate editor of Discrete Applied Mathematics. He teaches Discrete Mathematics courses and has supervised 14 Ph.D. students. In 2007 he received the Slovenian national award for highest achievements in mathematics.

Primož Potočnik

Primož Potočnik works in the field of symmetries of combinatorial objects. He has graduated in 2000 from University of Ljubljana and continued his postdoctoral education in Ottawa (Canada), Auckland (New Zealand), and Flagstaff (USA) as a Fullbright fellow. He has written over 50 scientific papers, published a textbook on graph theory and combinatorics, and supervised two PhD students.

Dušan Repovš

Professor Dušan Repovš obtained his PhD at the Florida State University in 1983. His research is mostly in topology. He has published over 300 papers and 2 monographs. He has also given over 400 invited talks around the world. He has been elected to several learned societies and has received awards at home and abroad. He teaches various courses in topology and has written a university textbook.

Riste Škrekovski

Riste Škrekovski got his PhD degree in graph colorings under the supervision of Bojan Mohar in 2000 at the University of Ljubljana. His main research interest are various topics in classical graph theory, but he is also interested in applied and interdisciplinary areas as chemical mathematics, complex networks, metaheuristics... He has published over 100 research papers. He is the leader of the research group in FIŠ, Novo Mesto.
